Gazpacho

JA
Inspired by José AndrésMy Thermomix reimagining of their gazpacho

My Thermomix take on José Andrés' silky Spanish gazpacho — a cold soup blitzed in seconds.

TM6 / TM7⏱️ Prep 10 min🍳 Cook 0 min🍽️ Serves 4📊 Lunch

No cooking at all — just ripe summer veg blitzed until glassy and smooth. The Thermomix turns a handful of tomatoes into the silkiest cold soup.

Ingredients

  • 🍅700 g ripe tomatoes
  • 🥒1 cucumber, peeled
  • 🫑1 red capsicum, deseeded
  • 🧄1 garlic clove
  • 🍷30 g sherry vinegar
  • 🌾40 g stale bread
  • 🧂1 tsp salt
  • 🫒80 g olive oil

Thermomix method

  1. Place the tomatoes, cucumber, capsicum, garlic, vinegar, bread and salt in the mixing bowl. Blend 30 sec / speed 7.
  2. With the blades running on speed 5, drizzle the olive oil through the lid, then blend 1 min / speed 9 until completely silky.
  3. Chill at least 1 hour. Check the seasoning and serve cold with an extra drizzle of olive oil

Aly's tips

  • The best tomatoes you can find make the whole dish.
  • For an ultra-smooth finish, pass it through a sieve.

Questions

Do you cook gazpacho?

No — it's a raw, cold soup. The Thermomix just blends it silky.

Why add bread?

It gives the soup body and that classic velvety texture.

Is this the original José Andrés recipe?

No. This is Chef Aly’s independent Thermomix reinterpretation inspired by José Andrés and gazpacho; it is not endorsed by or affiliated with the chef.
