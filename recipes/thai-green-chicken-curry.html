Thai Green Chicken Curry

A fragrant green chicken curry with rice steamed at the same time in the Varoma — a whole dinner in one machine.

TM6 / TM7⏱️ Prep 15 min🍳 Cook 25 min🍽️ Serves 4📊 Dinner

This is the Thermomix doing what it does best — cooking your whole dinner at once. The curry simmers in the bowl while the rice steams above it in the Varoma. One machine, one go, dinner done.

Ingredients

  • 1 brown onion, halved
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 20 g fresh ginger
  • 20 g vegetable oil
  • 2 tbsp Thai green curry paste
  • 600 g chicken thigh, diced
  • 400 g coconut milk (1 tin)
  • 100 g chicken stock
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp brown sugar
  • 150 g green beans, trimmed
  • Fresh coriander, basil and lime, to serve
  • For the rice: 300 g jasmine rice, rinsed + 300 g water

Thermomix method

  1. Place onion, garlic and ginger in the mixing bowl and chop 4 sec / speed 5. Scrape down.
  2. Add oil and the curry paste and sauté 3 min / 120°C / speed 1.
  3. Add coconut milk, stock, fish sauce, sugar and the diced chicken to the bowl. Put the rinsed rice and 300 g water into the Varoma dish and set the Varoma in position.
  4. Cook 20 min / Varoma / Reverse / speed 1 — the curry simmers below while the rice steams above.
  5. Add the green beans to the Varoma tray for the last 6 minutes (or straight into the bowl) so they stay crisp.
  6. Stir the curry, check the chicken is cooked through, and finish with coriander, basil and a squeeze of lime. Serve over the steamed rice.

Aly's tips

  • Use Reverse speed 1 so the chicken pieces stay whole and don't shred.
  • Like more heat? Add a sliced red chilli with the curry paste.
  • This Varoma 'dinner + rice at once' trick works for dozens of curries and stews.

Questions

Can the Thermomix cook curry and rice at the same time?

Yes — the curry cooks in the bowl while the rice steams in the Varoma above it, all in one 20-minute step.

Can I use chicken breast instead of thigh?

You can, but thigh stays juicier during the longer cook. If using breast, dice it a little larger.
