Brazilian Brigadeiro

Classic Brazilian chocolate fudge balls made effortlessly in the Thermomix — Aly's family recipe.

TM6 / TM7⏱️ Prep 5 min🍳 Cook 12 min🍽️ Serves 20📊 Dessert

A little taste of Brazil. Brigadeiro is the sweet at every Brazilian party, and the Thermomix takes away all the arm work — no constant stirring over a hot pan. This is the recipe Aly grew up with.

Ingredients

  • 395 g sweetened condensed milk (1 tin)
  • 30 g cocoa powder, sifted
  • 20 g butter, plus extra for your hands
  • 1 pinch salt
  • Chocolate sprinkles, to roll

Thermomix method

  1. Place condensed milk, cocoa, butter and salt in the mixing bowl.
  2. Cook 12 min / 100°C / speed 2, with the measuring cup off and the simmering basket on the lid to catch any splatter. It is ready when the mixture is thick and pulls away from the base.
  3. Pour onto a buttered plate and let it cool completely, about 30 minutes.
  4. With buttered hands, roll teaspoons of the mixture into small balls.
  5. Roll each ball in chocolate sprinkles and place in paper cases.

Aly's tips

  • Cook 1–2 minutes longer for firmer balls that hold their shape; less for a softer 'brigadeiro de colher' you eat with a spoon.
  • Swap sprinkles for crushed pistachios or desiccated coconut for a twist.
  • They keep 4–5 days in an airtight container.

Questions

Do I need to stir brigadeiro in the Thermomix?

No. At speed 2 the blade stirs it for you the whole time, so you skip the constant stirring the stovetop version needs.

How do I know when brigadeiro is done?

It is ready when it thickens and pulls cleanly away from the base of the bowl — around 12 minutes at 100°C.
